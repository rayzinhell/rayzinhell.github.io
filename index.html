── ⚓ Ship's Log ⚓ ──

Twenty years and more have I sailed the waters of software and systems, commanding crews and charting courses through the depths of defense technology. From the first crude maps to the complex architectures of today, the work has never been merely work — it has been an expedition, and I its captain.

My present commission is among the most singular in the fleet: I oversee the design, development, testing, and deployment of autonomous undersea vehicles — machines that dive alone into the abyss, guided by nothing but the intelligence we build into them. Ghost machines. They hunt the deep without lantern or crew, and it is our craft that gives them purpose.

When not at the helm, I am found where the horizon is wide — outdoors, always moving, always reading. A sailor who studies the charts before setting sail; a builder who learns before building. The sea rewards preparation and punishes arrogance. I have not forgotten this.
