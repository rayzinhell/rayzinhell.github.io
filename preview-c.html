About

Twenty-odd years into a career building software for people who operate in places most never see, I've had the good fortune to work on systems that genuinely matter. My focus has been defense — specifically autonomous undersea vehicles, where software touches everything: mission planning, real-time autonomy, sensor integration, and the quiet confidence a crew needs when something is operating beneath the surface without them. I've been through the full lifecycle more times than I can count — design, development, testing, field deployment — and I still find it interesting.

Outside of work, I spend time outdoors whenever I can and almost always have a book going. I've come to believe that curiosity and kindness are genuinely the two most useful professional skills a person can develop — more than any framework, language, or methodology. Curious people figure things out. Kind people build teams worth being on.

"I've never regretted being kind to someone."
